The Problem

Technical interviews were designed for a different era.

Engineers ship with AI every day. Most hiring processes still pretend they don't.

01 — the gap

AI changed the job, but not the interview.

Every engineer uses AI to ship — for boilerplate, debugging, architecture decisions. Most assessments either ban AI entirely or can't measure how it was used. You're testing for a job that doesn't exist anymore.

02 — the burnout

Take-homes don't scale, and interviewers burn out.

Pairing an engineer with every candidate is the gold standard — but it doesn't scale. So teams fall back on algorithmic puzzles that tell you nothing about how someone actually builds.

03 — the blindspot

You see the output. You never see the process.

Did they architect the solution or paste it from ChatGPT? Did they iterate thoughtfully or thrash? Today's assessments give you a score. They don't give you understanding.

Our position

Using AI isn’t the problem.
Pretending you didn’t is.

Good code is not defined by whether someone types every line by hand. Great engineers are defined by how they design, architect, decompose, and use the right tools to ship something that holds up under load.

In production, AI assistants are part of the ecosystem. Cursor, Claude Code, Copilot, Windsurf, Cody — they make good engineers faster and let teams focus on the parts that actually matter. We use them ourselves. So do your candidates.

Interviews are different. The point of an assessment isn’t to ban tools — it’s to surface the human underneath them. Can they reason about a system? Choose the right abstraction? Debug under pressure? Tell you why they did what they did?

So we don’t block. We attribute. Every line of code in every session is tagged: human, AI, AI-modified-by-human, or pasted. You decide what’s allowed for the role. We give you the full picture, line by line, and the AI debrief that proves they understand what they shipped.

“Don’t test whether they used AI. Test whether they could have built it without.”

The Monitoring Stack

Eight signal layers. One sealed session.

Web tracking is a surface. Proctored One operates at the OS, network, behavior, and code layers — so every keystroke is contextualized, every event is timestamped, and every session is replay-ready for audit.

01 · SYSTEM

System logs & events

Process spawn, network egress, USB attach, clipboard transitions — the entire syscall surface, captured.

02 · CAMERA

Camera & presence

Continuous identity verification, gaze tracking, off-camera alerts, second-person detection.

03 · SCREEN

Screen & share activity

Full screen recording, secondary display detection, screen-share to external apps blocked or flagged.

04 · BEHAVIOR

Behavior patterns

Keystroke cadence, paste-burst detection, focus drift, anomaly scoring against the candidate's baseline.

05 · ACCESS

App access policies

Allowlist only the apps the role requires — Cursor, VS Code, terminal. Everything else is sealed at the OS.

06 · AI USAGE

AI tool attribution

Every prompt, accept, reject — line-by-line. Human / AI-generated / AI-modified, with confidence intervals.

07 · NETWORK

Network & egress

DNS allowlists, exfil detection, packet-level audit of any external service the workspace touches.

08 · AUDIT

Compliance & audit log

Tamper-evident, hash-chained event log. SOC 2, ISO 27001, GDPR, EEOC — export-ready in one click.
